\subsection{FRM Exam 2007: \(\Var(3A+2B)\) with \(\mathrm{Cov}(A,B)=0.35\)}

\textbf{Enunciado}

\emph{Suppose that \(A\) and \(B\) are random variables, each follows a standard normal distribution, and the covariance between \(A\) and \(B\) is 0.35. What is the variance of \((3A+2B)\)?}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\alph*.]
\item 14.47
\item \hl{17.20}
\item 9.20
\item 15.10
\end{enumerate}

\subsubsection*{Solución}

Para constantes \(a,b\):
\[
\Var(aA+bB)=a^2\Var(A)+b^2\Var(B)+2ab\,\mathrm{Cov}(A,B).
\]


Como \(A,B\) son normales estándar:
\[
\Var(A)=1,\quad \Var(B)=1,\quad \mathrm{Cov}(A,B)=0.35.
\]
Con \(a=3\), \(b=2\):
\[
\Var(3A+2B)=9(1)+4(1)+2(3)(2)(0.35)=13+4.2=17.2.
\]
