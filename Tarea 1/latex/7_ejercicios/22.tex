\subsection{FRM Exam 1999: relationship between normal and lognormal}

\textbf{Enunciado}

\emph{Which of the following statements best characterizes the relationship between the normal and lognormal distributions?}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\alph*.]
\item The lognormal distribution is the logarithm of the normal distribution.
\item If the natural log of the random variable X is lognormally distributed, then X is normally distributed.
\item \hl{If X is lognormally distributed, then the natural log of X is normally distributed.} 
\item The two distributions have nothing to do with one another.
\end{enumerate}

\subsubsection*{Solución}


Decimos que \(X\) es \textbf{lognormal} si existe \(Y\sim\mathcal N(\mu,\sigma^2)\) tal que:
\[
X=e^{Y}.
\]
Aplicando logaritmo natural:
\[
\ln(X)=Y\sim\mathcal N(\mu,\sigma^2).
\]
Es decir: \(\ln X\) es normal.


Si \(X\) es lognormal, entonces \(\ln(X)\) es normal, así que (c) es correcta.
