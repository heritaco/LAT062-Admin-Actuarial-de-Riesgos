\subsection{Backtesting: 1,000 días, VaR 99\%, 17 excepciones (prueba one-tailed 5\%)}

\textbf{Enunciado}

\emph{Suppose that we back-test a VaR model using 1,000 days of data. The VaR confidence level is 99\% and we observe 17 exceptions. Should we reject the model at the 5\% confidence level? Use a one-tailed test.}

\subsubsection*{Solución}

Sí, se rechaza al 5\% (one-tailed).


\textbf{Hipótesis nula. }Bajo \(H_0\), \(p=1-0.99=0.01\) y
\[
N\sim \mathrm{Binomial}(1000,0.01).
\]

\textbf{Valor-p (cola derecha)}\\
\[
p\text{-value}=\Prob(N\ge 17\mid H_0)=1-\Prob(N\le 16)\approx 0.0264.
\]


Como \(p\text{-value}<0.05\), se rechaza \(H_0\).
